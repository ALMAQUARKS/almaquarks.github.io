  <p class="quarks-kicker">Legacy</p>
  <h1>ATOMS Legacy</h1>
  <p>
    QUARKS grows out of the ATOMS Band 3 survey, carrying a well-defined sample of massive star-forming regions to the smaller physical scales probed by ALMA Band 6.
  </p>
</section>

<section class="quarks-info-grid quarks-info-grid--legacy" aria-label="ATOMS legacy highlights">
  <article class="quarks-info-card">
    <span>01</span>
    <h2>Band 3 foundation</h2>
    <p>ATOMS provides the broader molecular-line and continuum view of massive star-forming environments, setting the clump-scale context for the QUARKS follow-up.</p>
  </article>
  <article class="quarks-info-card">
    <span>02</span>
    <h2>Sample continuity</h2>
    <p>QUARKS follows dense protoclusters selected from the ATOMS sample, allowing source-by-source comparisons across wavelength, resolution, and physical scale.</p>
  </article>
  <article class="quarks-info-card">
    <span>03</span>
    <h2>Multi-scale science</h2>
    <p>Together, ATOMS and QUARKS connect clump structure, fragmentation, dense cores, chemistry, kinematics, accretion, outflows, and feedback.</p>
  </article>
</section>

<section class="quarks-story">
  <div>
    <p class="quarks-section__eyebrow">Context</p>
    <h2>From ATOMS to QUARKS</h2>
  </div>
  <div class="quarks-story__body">
    <p>
      ATOMS, the ALMA Three-millimeter Observations of Massive Star-forming regions survey, established a Band 3 view of active massive star-forming regions. It is the legacy reference for the larger-scale dense gas, molecular emission, and feedback environment in which massive protoclusters form.
    </p>
    <p>
      QUARKS builds on that foundation with high-resolution ALMA Band 6 observations. The combined data set is designed for direct multi-scale comparisons: from clump-scale structures in ATOMS to compact continuum fragments, dense cores, and local gas motions resolved by QUARKS.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>
