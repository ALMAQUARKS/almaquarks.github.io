# Legacy

## ATOMS as the foundation for QUARKS

ATOMS, the **ALMA Three-millimeter Observations of Massive Star-forming regions** survey, provides a Band 3 view of active massive star-forming regions. It established the large-scale context for dense gas structures, molecular line emission, feedback signatures, and the environments in which massive protoclusters form.

QUARKS builds on this legacy by following the target sample to higher angular resolution with ALMA Band 6 observations. Together, ATOMS and QUARKS connect clump-scale gas structures to protocluster-scale fragmentation, dense core formation, accretion flows, outflows, chemistry, and feedback.

## Why combine ATOMS and QUARKS?

- ATOMS provides the broader Band 3 view of massive star-forming environments.
- QUARKS resolves denser, smaller-scale Band 6 structures within the same evolutionary context.
- The combined data set enables source-by-source multi-scale comparisons across continuum and line products.
- The atlas products on this website are intended to make those comparisons easier to inspect and cite.
